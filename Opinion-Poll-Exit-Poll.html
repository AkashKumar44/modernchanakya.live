Opinion Poll / Exit Poll

What is the definition of opinion poll?

It is a survey or investigation conducted on a scientific method designed to clothe public opinion about a particular subject or range of subjects. Interview Interviewer questions people in your political field. With this, the opinion poll now shows an important role in politics. They are used by customers during election campaign to identify their image and by media to see which candidate is going to be in the election.

What is the opinion poll method?

Opinion poll is a way of collecting information about the people of a group or class. Opinion polls usually include a fair sample of respondents, who constitute 3% of the total population of an electoral constituency. And they are asked questions based on the activities of the people in the area. After that, a report is acted on based on the answers to the questions asked.

After the election announcement, such a survey is conducted in the constituency where voters are asked which party you can vote for. What are the major issues in your area and what is your opinion for various political parties. The survey is called Opinion Poll. Exit poll data tries to understand the mood of the voters after the election, with which views the voter has turned towards which party.

This survey mainly focuses on sample size. The larger the sample size, the more accurate the election results are. Many companies in India successfully do this kind of opinion poll, of which Modern Chanakya is one.
